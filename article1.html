What To Do When a Loved One With Dementia Gets Angry

I learned in caring for my grandmother that dementia causes fear and confusion.

In the beginning, my grandmother would say things that were not right and I would correct her. This would cause her to get frustrated, upset and angry.

I quickly learned this was not the correct approach as I was not helping her but getting her more frustrated and anxious.

When a loved one with dementia demonstrates anger and confusion, it is important to not contradict and to provide a level of comfort.

I would gently hold my grandmother's hand and talk to her. Later on, I discovered playing that her favorite songs were soothing to her and also made her happy. This led her to move and tap her feet in sitting position.

Although it may be sad to see a loved on with dementia deteriorate, it brought me comfort to bring her joy.
