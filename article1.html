What To Do When a Loved One With Dementia Gets Angry

I learned in caring for my grandmother that dementia causes fear and confusion.

In the beginning my grandmother would say things that were not right and I would contradict her and correct her.  This would cause her to get frustrated, upset and angry.

I quickly learned this was not the correct approach as I was not helping her but getting her more frustrated and anxious.

When a loved one with dementia demonstrates anger and confusion is important not to contract and to provide a level of comfort.  

I would gently hold my grandmother hand and talk to her.  Later on I discovered playing her favorite songs were soothing and also made her happy and led her to move and dance even in sitting position.

Although it may be sad to see a loved on with dementia deteriorate, it brought me comfort to bring her comfort and joy, hence the focus should be the loved one and bringing them comfort and joy.
