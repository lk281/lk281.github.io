Tips For Caring For Loved Ones With Dementia

Elderly Hispanic family members with dementia are more likely to live with their families than in elderly facilities. As such, if you are living and caring for a loved one with dementia, it is important to develop a comfortable environment, plan for care accordingly and be informed on best practices when interacting with your loved one.

Background: I cared for my elderly grandmother for two hours everyday after school. I was the intermediate help, between the time the home aid left and when my mother arrived home from work.

Tips

Provide a calm environment

Provide physical comfort

Create a routine to avoid confusion

Talk to the person

Play music to calm the person

Avoid having the television on, as this can cause confusion and anxiety

Never contradict the person

Provide comfort by speaking to them and listening to what they are saying

Providing a comfortable environment may require getting the following:

Walker

Cane

Transporter Wheel Chair

Clothes that can be easily put on (sweat pants, dresses, etc.)
